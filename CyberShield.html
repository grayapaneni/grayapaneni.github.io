01 · The problem
The people most at risk
have the fewest defenses

The average home now has 12 or more connected devices — cameras, thermostats, smart speakers, baby monitors. None of them have antivirus software. None send alerts. When a hacker quietly recruits a baby monitor into a botnet or starts watching through a camera, the owner never finds out. There is no alarm.

In public, Bluetooth-enabled skimmers hidden inside gas station pumps and ATMs harvest card numbers wirelessly. A $20 device operated from a car 30 feet away is completely invisible to the person tapping their card. Consumer security products address neither problem. CyberShield is built for this gap.

04 · Key findings
What SHAP revealed about
how IoT attacks actually look

SHAP interaction plots, PCA visualization, and feature importance analysis surfaced four findings with real implications for how the system gets deployed.

Finding 01

Magnitude is the top predictor

Packet size magnitude across a flow is the single strongest signal. Attack traffic has a fundamentally different size profile than benign traffic at the flow level.

Finding 02

IAT separates flood attacks instantly

Inter-Arrival Time is the second strongest feature. DDoS and DoS attacks have near-zero packet intervals — a near-perfect discriminator with a single feature.

Finding 03

15+ features are redundant

Correlation matrix analysis identified over 15 features carrying duplicate information — a clear path to a lighter mobile model without meaningful accuracy loss.

Finding 04

BruteForce leaks into Recon

BruteForce misclassifies as Recon (9%) and Web (7%) due to structural similarity at the network flow level — a known open problem in the IoT security literature.

05 · System design
Built around one hard constraint:
nothing personal leaves the device

The privacy architecture isn't a feature — it's a design constraint that shaped every technical decision. The ML model runs on the Pi, not in the cloud. Bluetooth scanning runs on the phone, not a server. Community reports are anonymized on-device before transmission.

Tier 1 · Home network

Full XGBoost on Raspberry Pi 5

11.8MB model, all 46 features, full 99.56% accuracy. Pi 5 with 8GB RAM confirmed capable of running the complete model in real time from live tshark capture.

Tier 2 · Mobile

Distilled TFLite on-device

Under 1MB compressed model, retaining 97–98% accuracy. Runs entirely on the phone for Bluetooth skimmer detection — zero server calls during scanning.

DS decisions made
  • Smart sampling: loaded every 5th of 169 CSVs (34 files, 9.5M rows) to balance dataset coverage against memory constraints
  • Attack category mapping: collapsed 34 granular labels into 8 semantic categories — DDoS, DoS, Mirai, Recon, Spoofing, Web, BruteForce, Benign
  • Class imbalance handling: weighted XGBoost training to prevent Benign majority-class dominance over minority attack types
  • SHAP interaction analysis: confirmed Protocol Type as the most decisive feature, interacting strongly with every other top predictor
  • Hardware-aware model selection: chose XGBoost over neural approaches to enable direct Raspberry Pi deployment without GPU
  • Redundancy mapping: identified 15+ correlated features, documenting the optimization path for TFLite mobile distillation
